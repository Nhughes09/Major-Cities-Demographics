Data Sources & Methodology

This dashboard presents modeled projections of ethnic/racial composition for select global cities from 2025 to 2100. Projections are interpolated from baseline 2025 estimates and 2100 targets informed by:

  • UN World Population Prospects 2022 ‚Äî country and city population growth trajectories
  • UN World Urbanization Prospects 2025 ‚Äî urban population estimates
  • Pew Research Center ‚Äî US Hispanic population projections, racial/ethnic trends
  • Eurostat ‚Äî EU population projections, foreign-born population data
  • US Census Bureau ‚Äî racial and ethnic demographic projections
  • National statistical agencies ‚Äî UK ONS, Statistics Canada, ABS Australia, etc.

Important caveats: Precise ethnic composition projections at the city level do not exist from official sources for 2100. These projections are illustrative models based on observable macro-trends including:

  • Africa's population growth (1.5B ‚Üí ~3.8B by 2100)
  • European native population decline (EU ‚àí6% by 2100 with immigration)
  • US Hispanic population growth (~25% of US by 2050)
  • Increasing multiracial/mixed identification globally
  • Migration-driven diversification of global cities
  • Differential fertility rates across demographic groups

Ethnic categories are simplified and vary by regional context. Actual demographic change will be influenced by policy, economics, climate, and social factors that cannot be predicted with certainty.
